Post-Restoration Instructions
for Pediatric Patients

1 Numbness After Treatment

  • Your child's lips, cheek, and tongue may remain numb for 1–3 hours.
  • Encourage your child not to bite, chew, suck, or scratch the numb area.
  • Closely supervise young children until numbness wears off.

2 Discomfort or Sensitivity

  • Mild sensitivity to cold, heat, or pressure is normal for a few days.
  • Mild soreness at injection sites may occur.
  • Use child-safe pain relief only if advised by your dentist.

3 Eating & Drinking

  • Avoid eating until numbness wears off.
  • Soft foods are recommended for the first few hours.
  • Avoid crunchy, sticky, or hard foods.
  • Encourage plenty of water.

4 Silver (Amalgam) Fillings

  • Avoid chewing on that side for 24 hours.
  • Avoid sticky foods like gum or caramel.

5 Tooth-Colored (Composite) Fillings

  • The filling sets immediately.
  • Normal eating can resume after numbness wears off.

6 Crowns on Baby Teeth

  • The crown may feel "high" for a day or two.
  • Avoid sticky foods.
  • If the crown comes off, keep it safely and contact the clinic.

7 Oral Habits to Avoid

  • No biting pencils, nails, or ice.
  • Prevent children from chewing toys or fingers while numb.

8 When to Contact the Dentist

  • Persistent pain after 48 hours
  • Swelling
  • Increasing sensitivity
  • Loose or broken filling/crown
  • Lip or cheek biting causing swelling or infection

Post–Root Canal Treatment
Instructions for Children

1 Numbness After the Appointment

  • Lips, cheeks, and tongue may remain numb for 1–3 hours.
  • Avoid biting, chewing, sucking, or scratching numb areas.
  • Closely supervise your child.

2 Pain or Discomfort

  • Mild discomfort for 1–2 days is normal.
  • Use child-safe pain relief only if recommended.
  • Sensitivity while chewing may occur temporarily.

3 Eating Instructions

  • Avoid eating until numbness wears off.
  • Eat soft foods for 24 hours.
  • Avoid crunchy, sticky, or hard foods.
  • Encourage plenty of fluids.

4 Crown Placement Reminder

  • Root canal–treated baby teeth usually require crowns.
  • Avoid sticky foods after crown placement.
  • The bite may feel different temporarily.

5 Oral Hygiene

  • Continue brushing twice daily.
  • Brush gently around the treated area.
  • Avoid poking the tooth.

6 Activity Restrictions

  • Normal activities can resume.
  • Avoid intense activity for a few hours if tired or sore.

7 Contact the Dentist If

  • Severe pain continues after 48 hours
  • Swelling or fever develops
  • Pus or bad smell occurs
  • The crown or filling becomes loose

Orthodontic Treatment
What to Expect

1 What Orthodontic Treatment Helps Correct

  • Crooked or crowded teeth
  • Gaps between teeth
  • Overbites, underbites, and crossbites
  • Jaw alignment issues

2 Types of Treatment

  • Metal braces
  • Ceramic braces
  • Clear aligners
  • Functional appliances
  • Retainers

3 First Few Days

  • Mild soreness or tightness is normal.
  • Soft foods are recommended.
  • Minor irritation inside the mouth may occur.

4 Eating With Braces

  • Avoid sticky and hard foods.
  • Cut hard fruits into small pieces.
  • Aligners should be removed before meals.

5 Oral Hygiene

  • Brush after every meal.
  • Use orthodontic or interdental brushes.
  • Clean aligners daily.

6 Retainers

  • Wear retainers exactly as instructed.
  • Skipping retainers may cause teeth to shift back.

7 Contact Your Orthodontist If

  • A wire or bracket breaks
  • Aligners crack or stop fitting
  • Severe pain or swelling occurs

Post-Implant Surgery
Instructions

1 Immediately After Surgery

  • Bite gently on the gauze pack for 30–45 minutes.
  • Mild oozing is normal during the first 24 hours.
  • Avoid disturbing the surgical area.

2 Swelling & Pain

  • Apply ice packs 15 minutes on/off.
  • Keep your head elevated.
  • Take medications as prescribed.

3 Diet

  • Eat soft foods for 24–48 hours.
  • Avoid spicy, crunchy, or hard foods.
  • Do not use straws.

4 Oral Hygiene

  • Do not rinse for 24 hours.
  • Use warm salt water rinses after 24 hours.
  • Brush carefully around the area.

5 Contact the Clinic If

  • Bleeding does not stop
  • Swelling or pain worsens
  • You notice discharge or implant looseness

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about dental treatments and our clinic.

How often should I visit the dentist?

We recommend visiting the dentist every six months for a routine check-up and cleaning. However, some patients with specific conditions may need to visit more frequently as recommended by their dentist.

Are dental X-rays safe?

Yes, digital X-rays use very low radiation levels and are completely safe when performed under professional supervision.

What is the right age for braces?

Braces can be done at almost any age, but the ideal time is between 10–14 years when teeth and jaws are still developing.

How long do dental implants last?

With proper care, dental implants can last 15–20 years or even a lifetime.

Does teeth whitening damage enamel?

No, professional teeth whitening is safe and does not harm enamel when performed under a dentist's supervision.
